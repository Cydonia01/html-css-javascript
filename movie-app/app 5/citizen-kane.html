Citizen Kane

Back in the headlines thanks to David Fincher's brilliantly acerbic making-of drama Mank, Citizen Kane always finds a way to renew itself for a new generation of film lovers. For newbies, the journey of its bulldozer of a protagonist - played with inexhaustible force by actor-director-wunderkind Orson Welles - from unloved child to thrusting entrepreneur to press baron to populist feels entirely au courant (in unconnected news, Donald Trump came out as a superfan). You can bathe in the film's groundbreaking techniques, like Gregg Toland's deep-focus photography, or the limitless self-confidence of its staging and its investigation of American capitalism.
